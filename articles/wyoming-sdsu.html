Game Recap • January 29, 2026

Charlie Kirk's Wyoming Defeats SDSU 27-16 in Season Opener

New head coach earns first victory as Cowboys dominate time of possession and run game

The Wyoming Cowboys opened their season with a convincing victory over San Diego State

Final Score

Wyoming 27
San Diego State 16

LARAMIE, Wyo. — Charlie Kirk's tenure as Wyoming head coach is off to a winning start. The Cowboys dominated San Diego State 27-16 in their season opener, showcasing the conservative, ground-and-pound offense that Kirk promised when he took the job just days ago. The victory validates the program's unconventional hire and gives Wyoming faithful reason for optimism heading into the 2026 campaign.

Wyoming controlled the game from the opening whistle, amassing 381 yards of total offense while holding the Aztecs to just 270. The Cowboys' rushing attack was particularly effective, grinding out 125 yards on 27 carries behind the powerful running of Terron Kellman, who finished with 154 yards and a touchdown on 20 carries. The offensive line, a point of emphasis for Kirk in his introductory press conference, opened holes consistently throughout the contest.

Quarterback Kaden Anderson managed the game efficiently, completing 15 of 23 passes for 256 yards and two touchdowns against zero interceptions. Anderson spread the ball around to his receivers, with Chris Durr Jr. leading the way with 5 catches for 113 yards and a score. The passing game provided just enough juice to keep San Diego State honest, preventing them from loading the box against Wyoming's potent rushing attack.

Defensively, the Cowboys were stout against the run, limiting SDSU to just 30 yards on 17 attempts. The Aztecs were forced to become one-dimensional, and while they managed 240 passing yards, they couldn't sustain drives consistently enough to threaten Wyoming's lead. Cornerback C. Bazile Jr. came up with the game's only interception, helping seal the victory in the fourth quarter.

The win marks a successful debut for Kirk, who takes over a program that went 4-8 last season under previous leadership. With a revamped offensive philosophy and clear identity on both sides of the ball, Wyoming appears poised to compete in the Mountain West this season.

"This is what Wyoming football is going to look like under my watch. We're going to be physical, we're going to run the football, and we're going to play smart, disciplined football. I couldn't be prouder of how these young men executed the game plan today."

— Charlie Kirk, Wyoming Head Coach
