"The Game Was Stolen From Us, Just Like the Election": Kirk Melts Down as NDSU Falls to Unranked New Mexico

CU
By CFB Updates Staff July 6, 2026 · 8:10 PM ET
5 min read

It was supposed to be a coronation. North Dakota State, ranked No. 24 to open Season 2 after last year's storybook FBS debut, hosted an unranked New Mexico squad that nobody in Fargo expected to hang around. Instead, the Bison authored one of the most baffling losses of the young season — outgaining New Mexico 367 to 257, piling up 21 first downs to 11, and still walking off the field on the wrong end of a 23-16 final.

NDSU did just about everything but the one thing that matters. They moved the ball at will, controlled the clock, and dominated the box score. They also turned it over twice through the air, stalled repeatedly in the red zone, and spotted the Lobos a 15-0 halftime lead they could never fully erase. A furious second-half rally — 16 unanswered-feeling points — came up a touchdown short.

Final — Season Opener (at Fargo)

New Mexico 23, NDSU 16 — NDSU (No. 24) falls at home

Scoring by quarter — UNM: 3 · 6 · 6 · 8  |  NDSU: 0 · 0 · 7 · 9

And then came the podium. Coach Charlie Kirk, jaw tight, eyes red, stepped to the microphone and delivered a postgame statement that had less to do with third-down efficiency than with, apparently, democracy itself.

"The game was stolen from us, just like the election. We need to get to the bottom of this."

"You look at the numbers. You LOOK at them," Kirk said, slapping a stat sheet against the lectern. "Three hundred sixty-seven yards to two hundred fifty-seven. Twenty-one first downs to eleven. We won this football game by every honest metric known to man. And somehow — SOMEHOW — the scoreboard says we lost. Explain that. You can't. Because it wasn't real. This result will not be certified."

Asked whether the two interceptions thrown by quarterback T. Reynolds might have played a role, Kirk waved the question away. "Those weren't interceptions. Those were ballots. Fake ballots. Somebody got into the machine. I want a forensic audit of all 28 pass attempts, and I want it by morning."

367 – 257 NDSU outgained New Mexico by 110 yards — and lost anyway

The numbers tell the story of a team that beat itself. Reynolds went 17-of-28 for 227 yards and a touchdown, but the two picks were the difference. Reigning Heisman winner DJ Scott, so dominant a year ago, was bottled up for just 80 yards on 17 carries (he did add a score and three catches for 51). New Mexico, meanwhile, did more with less: quarterback A. Odom went 13-of-23 for 194 and a touchdown despite being sacked four times, and running back T. Jones churned out 84 tough yards and a score. Lobos safety J. Davis was everywhere — 11 tackles and one of the two backbreaking interceptions.

Scott, visibly frustrated in the locker room afterward, offered his own theory for the Heisman-caliber no-show — and it rhymed with his head coach's.

"Them boys was reading our playbook dead ass." — DJ Scott

"I'm not saying nothing crazy, I'm just saying they knew," Scott continued, shaking his head. "Every run I got, they was already there. Every route, sitting on it. You can't tell me that's football. That's espionage. Somebody sold us out." Pressed on whether the offense was simply out-executed, Scott repeated, flatly: "Dead ass. Reading. The playbook."

The most concise assessment of the evening, however, came from NDSU assistant coach Kim Jong Un, who leaned into a nearby microphone unprompted, surveyed the stat sheet, and rendered his verdict with the gravity of a man who has personally overseen many investigations.

"ITS VERUH SKETCHY. NOT VERUH GUH AT AH." — Kim Jong Un

"We win the game. Look at paper. Paper say we win. Scoreboard say we rose. Cannot be both," Un explained, tapping the totals. "In my experience, when paper and scoreboard disagree, is the scoreboard who is rying. I have seen this before. We will find out who. They will be VERUH sorry. Not veruh guh for them at ah."

NDSU's athletic department has not announced any formal appeal, in part because no such appeal exists in the rules of college football. Kirk, undeterred, said he has already contacted "the proper authorities" and would be "filing paperwork with several agencies, at least one of which is real." The Bison fall to 0-1 to open Season 2, dropping their coronation before it began. New Mexico, efficient and unbothered, took the win and went home.

The result, per Coach Kirk, remains "under review, and will be under review until the correct outcome is achieved." 🏈🦬
