NDSU's Charlie Kirk and Texas State's Alex Jones Launch "Alien Capitol Research Fund" to Finance Groundbreaking Studies on How Shape-Shifting Globalist Overlords From the 4th Dimension Are Possessing Politicians and Running the New World Order — "I'm Gonna Be Honest, I'm Kind of Retarded," Says Alex Jones

By CFB Updates Staff July 6, 2026 · 10:45 PM ET
6 min read

College football has seen strange bedfellows before. It has never seen this. In a joint press conference that defied conference lines, common sense, and several laws of physics, North Dakota State head coach Charlie Kirk and Texas State head coach Alex Jones — sworn rivals, opposite ends of the map — emerged arm-in-arm Sunday night to announce the formation of a new charitable foundation: the Alien Capitol Research Fund.

Its mission, per the two coaches: to scientifically prove that the political elites running Washington, D.C. are not human beings at all, but shape-shifting interdimensional lizard overlords from the 4th dimension who are possessing politicians to operate the New World Order.

The two men embraced at the podium for a full ninety seconds before either spoke. The room did not know how to react. Neither did we.

"The Reptilians Have Infiltrated the RED ZONE"

Jones, drenched in sweat under the studio lights, opened the proceedings by slamming a jar of "brain-force supplements" onto the podium hard enough to make the microphone jump.

"Folks. FOLKS. I have the DOCUMENTS," Jones bellowed, eyes bulging. "The people running this country are NOT people. They are 12-foot inter-dimensional REPTILIANS from the 4th dimension, and they are wearing the skin of your senators like a Halloween costume. And now — NOW — they've infiltrated the RED ZONE. Why do you think the refs are so bad? BECAUSE THEY'RE LIZARDS, folks!"

He paused, wiped his brow, and lowered his voice to a near-whisper.

"I'm gonna be honest, I'm kind of retarded. But I'm RIGHT. And that's what scares them."— Alex Jones

The admission drew stunned silence from the assembled press, followed by scattered applause from a man in the back holding a "1776" flag and an air horn.

What the Fund Actually Does

According to the foundation's hastily-printed one-page charter — laminated, Jones noted, "so the shape-shifters can't dissolve it with their acid saliva" — the Alien Capitol Research Fund will finance "groundbreaking, Nobel-level, totally real science" into the following areas:

Alien Capitol Research Fund — 2026 Grant Priorities

1. Thermal-imaging every politician's forked tongue at the State of the Union

2. Proving the Capitol dome is a "lizard incubation chamber"

3. Determining why "they turned the freakin' FROGS 4th-dimensional"

4. A scholarship for "any linebacker brave enough to tackle a globalist"

5. Bulk filtered spring water & raw testosterone (operational costs)

Kirk, for his part, stood beside Jones nodding solemnly, occasionally interjecting with references to his own recent ayahuasca rebirth and thanking "fans, God, and Israel" for making the partnership possible.

"I came back from that ceremony a new man, and the new man SEES the lizards clearly now," Kirk said. "When Alex called me and said 'the Capitol is a terrarium,' something in my reborn soul just... knew. We may coach against each other. But against the reptilian New World Order? We are TEAMMATES."

The Football Connection Nobody Asked For

Pressed by a reporter on what, exactly, any of this had to do with college football, Jones appeared genuinely offended by the question.

"What does it have to do with football? EVERYTHING, sweetheart," he snapped, gesturing wildly. "You think it's a COINCIDENCE my Bobcats only have three running backs? The lizards did that. You think Kirk's Bison got that anonymous NIL money by ACCIDENT? That was reptilian seed capital, folks, and we are gonna use it to fund the RESISTANCE."

He leaned into the microphone, the veins in his neck now clearly visible.

"They put the fluoride in the Gatorade. They put 5G in the wristband playbooks. They turned my third-string tight end into what I can only describe as 'compliant.' NOT ON MY WATCH. The Alien Capitol Research Fund is gonna EXPOSE them, and we're gonna do it with SCIENCE — real science, the kind they don't teach you at their globalist universities."

Reactions Around the Sport

The reaction across college football ranged from concern to bewilderment to, in a few cases, checkbook-opening enthusiasm.

"I have read the charter four times," said one athletic director. "I still don't know if this is a charity, a cult, or a bit. But the merch is already outselling our bowl gear, so I've stopped asking questions."

A rival coach texted our staff simply: "Two head coaches. One embrace. Ninety seconds. I will never unsee it. God help this league."

What Comes Next

As the press conference stretched past the two-hour mark, Jones announced the fund's first "field study" would take place at NDSU's home opener, where volunteers armed with thermal cameras and "truth" will attempt to document any reptilian activity in the officiating crew.

"We are DRAINING the terrarium, folks," Jones declared, as Kirk pulled him into yet another embrace and the eagle — somehow present at an indoor facility — took flight. "They wanted us divided by conference. Instead we're UNITED by truth. The New World Order is TERRIFIED. And they should be."
