Thad Castle on Coach Kirk's Massive Knowledge Jump and Muscle Gain: "HE'S CHEATING, I F***ING KNOW IT. IT'S ISRAEL AND BIBI"

CU
By CFB Updates Staff July 2, 2026 · 10:15 PM ET
4 min read

Something is different about Charlie Kirk, and the rest of the league has noticed.

In the span of a single offseason, the NDSU head coach — a man who had never played a down of football in his life — has reportedly packed on 50 pounds of lean muscle, memorized entire pro-style offensive and defensive schemes, installed a full playbook from scratch, and developed the football IQ of a 30-year veteran. Coaches around the league went from confused, to suspicious, to openly accusatory.

Leading the charge: Colorado State's Thad Castle, who did not so much hold a press conference as detonate one.

"HE'S CHEATING. I F***ING KNOW IT. IT'S ISRAEL AND BIBI."— Coach Thad Castle, CSU

Castle, sweating through a mesh tank top and gripping what he described as "a totally legal energy beverage," continued his tirade for several uninterrupted minutes.

Press Conference Transcript

Q: Coach Castle, you're accusing Kirk of cheating. What's your evidence?

"Evidence? LOOK AT HIM. The guy couldn't spell 'football' in January and now he's drawing up Cover-2 robber schemes on a napkin. HE'S ON MORE THAN ADDY. THAT'S SOME CRAZY ISRAEL SHIT. Nobody gets that jacked AND that smart without help. NOBODY. I would know. I've tried BOTH."

Q: Coach Jones, you've been vocal about this too. What's your theory?

"IT'S THE MOSSAD, folks. He's working with the MOSSAD to harvest adrenochrome. They call it 'Coaching Points' — C.P. The Mossad is providing him with the C.P. You watch the film, you'll see it, the man BLINKS in Hebrew now. Wake up! It's all in the playbook wristbands!"

Q: Coach Un, any comment on the allegations against your head coach?

"Oh, Kirk arways reary strong man. Very smart, very big. Is normar. Everybody grow 50 pound in my country too, is Tuesday. Go Bison."

Kirk Responds

Reached for comment, Kirk was — for a moment — the picture of calm.

"Guys, Bibi has been providing me with some protein shakes and books. Relax," Kirk said, before the tone shifted abruptly. "We just lost to Wyoming, who — mind you — were THUGS. THEY HAD NO IQ. JUST RELIED ON RAW TALENT—"

At this point, sources say a visible wave of what witnesses could only describe as sudden roid rage washed over the coach. He gripped the podium, exhaled through his nose for a full ten seconds, and appeared to physically restrain himself.

"…I mean. Nice guys. Yeah. For sure," Kirk continued, voice suddenly gentle. "Anyways — tell that cokehead and that conspiracy nutjob to chill out. If they want some of the good shit, they know where to find it."

Kirk then calmly produced a yarmulke from his jacket pocket, placed it on his head, and walked away without another word.

"If they want some of the good shit, they know where to find it."— Coach Charlie Kirk, NDSU (3-0)

The League Reacts

Bibi Netanyahu, spotted seated calmly in the corner of the NDSU locker room throughout the ordeal, declined to comment but was observed nodding approvingly and sipping from a shaker bottle labeled "GAINS (KOSHER)."

Castle, undeterred, promised to "get to the bottom of this" by "eating the same books Kirk ate." Alex Jones has reportedly filed a FOIA request with the Missouri Valley Football Conference. And Coach Un continues to insist everything is completely normal.

The Bison, for whatever it's worth, are 3-0. 🏈
