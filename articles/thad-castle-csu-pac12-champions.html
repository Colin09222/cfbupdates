"HOLD YOUR COCKS" — Thad Castle Storms Fort Collins to a PAC-12 Title in Year One

CU
By CFB Updates Staff July 6, 2026 · 8:30 AM ET
4 min read

Remember when they said he only wins press conferences? Somebody go find those people. Coach Thad Castle took the Colorado State job, looked the entire PAC-12 dead in the eye, and dragged the Rams to a 12-1 record, a perfect 8-0 in conference play, and a 28-17 conference championship victory over Boise State — all in his first season in Fort Collins.

Altitude, meet the Head Ball Coach. The man who said "elevation is my sixth man, baby" wasn't kidding. Colorado State didn't just compete in Year One — they conquered.

PAC-12 Championship — Final

Colorado State 28 · Boise State 17

Regular Season: 12-1 overall · 8-0 conference

Head Coach: Thad Castle (Year 1)

8-0 Colorado State's conference record — a perfect run through the PAC-12

The Perfect Run

Eight conference games. Eight wins. Not a single blemish inside the league. The lone loss on the season only sharpened the Rams, and by the time championship weekend rolled around, Colorado State looked less like a first-year project and more like a program that had been building toward this for a decade.

Castle preached one thing all year, screamed at maximum volume in the locker room before every kickoff: "Hold your cocks and play football!" Somehow — improbably, beautifully — it worked.

"They told me it was the longest road of the four jobs. GOOD. I like long roads. More time to run people over."— Thad Castle

The Championship: Rams 28, Broncos 17

Boise State came in dangerous, but the blue turf couldn't save them from the Fort Collins freight train. Colorado State controlled the line of scrimmage, leaned on the ground game exactly the way Castle drew it up, and pulled away in the second half. Eleven-point final margin. A conference trophy hoisted at altitude.

When the clock hit zero, Castle reportedly sprinted the length of the sideline, tackled his own defensive coordinator, and screamed "HALF BACK TOSS" at a referee for no reason whatsoever.

Press Conference Transcript

Q: Coach, a PAC-12 title in Year One. Did you ever doubt this team?

"Doubt? DOUBT? Fellas, I showed up to my introduction with a beer from every school and picked the one at altitude on purpose. I don't doubt. I ELEVATE. Twelve and one, baby. Write that down. Write down the ONE loss too so it stays mad."

Q: What was the difference against Boise State tonight?

"Their turf is blue. Ours is a mile in the sky. You come up here breathin' thin air and try to tackle my backs? Good luck. We ran that HALF BACK TOSS till they filed a complaint with the league office."

Q: A message for the doubters?

"I'm not gonna threaten anybody at a press conference. That would be inappropriate. I'm just sayin' — I know where the doubters LIVE. And I'll be droppin' off a commemorative championship bottle. Personally. With a smile. Anyway — great season, love you guys, PAC-12 CHAMPS!"

The Bottom Line

Four schools called last summer. Castle picked the empire-building project, the "slow burn, big payoff" job — and then refused to make it slow. He built the empire in one year.

Colorado State: 12-1. Perfect in conference. PAC-12 Champions. And the loudest program in the league just got a whole lot louder. The rest of the conference had better hold their cocks, buddehs — because Thad Castle is only getting started. 🏈🏆
