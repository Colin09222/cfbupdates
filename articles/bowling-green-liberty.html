Game Recap • January 29, 2026

Sean Combs and Drew Pyne Lead Bowling Green Over Liberty 34-17

Falcons quarterback throws five touchdowns in dominant performance as new head coach earns first career victory

Head coach Sean Combs directs his players during Bowling Green's victory over Liberty

Final Score

Bowling Green 34
Liberty 17

BOWLING GREEN, Ohio — The Sean Combs era at Bowling Green began with fireworks. The Falcons dismantled Liberty 34-17 behind a spectacular five-touchdown performance from quarterback Drew Pyne, giving the controversial new head coach a victory in his collegiate coaching debut. The win silenced critics who questioned the program's decision to hire Combs and provided a glimpse of what this offense could become under his leadership.

Pyne was masterful from start to finish, completing 19 of 33 passes for 338 yards and five touchdowns with just two interceptions. The senior signal-caller distributed the ball effectively to his talented receiving corps, led by tight end A. Boardingham who hauled in 6 catches for 147 yards and three scores. Brennan Ridley added 4 receptions for 93 yards and a touchdown, stretching the field and keeping Liberty's secondary on its heels all afternoon.

The Falcons' offense racked up 433 yards of total offense, averaging 8.2 yards per play in a performance that will surely turn heads around the MAC. Bowling Green's offensive line provided excellent protection, allowing Pyne time to work through his progressions and find open receivers downfield. The passing attack was complemented by a solid ground game, with the Falcons rushing for 95 yards on 20 carries.

Liberty's offense struggled to find any rhythm against Bowling Green's aggressive defense. Quarterback Ethan Vasko completed just 18 of 30 passes for 219 yards and one touchdown, while the Flames' ground game was held to 112 yards on 38 attempts—a pedestrian 2.9 yards per carry. Head coach Jamey Chadwell will have plenty to address in the film room after this disappointing performance.

For Combs, the victory represents validation of his unconventional path to coaching. The music mogul has brought energy and attention to a program that often flies under the radar, and if performances like this continue, Bowling Green could emerge as a surprise contender in the Mid-American Conference.

"Drew Pyne was phenomenal today. Five touchdowns against a quality opponent—that's the kind of quarterback play that wins championships. I told these guys all week that if they executed, good things would happen. They believed, they executed, and we got the W."

— Sean Combs, Bowling Green Head Coach

Postgame Press Conference

Q: Coach, can you talk about what Drew Pyne means to this offense?

"Drew is the engine that makes everything go. He's a leader, he's accurate, and he makes plays when we need them. Five touchdowns speaks for itself. That's an elite performance."

Q: Coach Combs, there's been a lot of discussion about your recent presidential pardon. Do you have any comment on that situation and how it's affected your focus on the team?

"Look, I'm here to talk about football. That's what today is about—these young men and this victory. I'm not going to get into all that other stuff. Next question."

Q: But surely the circumstances surrounding—

"I said next question. We're here to talk about football. If you want to talk about the game, I'm happy to do that. Otherwise, we're done here."
