Sean "Diddy" Combs Hired as Head Coach of Bowling Green

Music mogul makes stunning transition to college football following recent presidential pardon

Sean Combs meets with Bowling Green staff during his introductory visit to the program

BOWLING GREEN, Ohio — In a hire that has sent shockwaves through the college football world, Bowling Green has named Sean "Diddy" Combs as its new head football coach. The music industry icon and entrepreneur, who recently received a presidential pardon from President Trump, is making an unprecedented leap into collegiate athletics with no prior coaching experience.

The Falcons parted ways with former head coach Scot Loeffler following a 5-7 campaign in 2024-25. The program struggled to find consistency in the Mid-American Conference, and athletic director Scott Brown indicated the department was looking for a "transformative figure" who could elevate Bowling Green's national profile while also competing on the field.

Combs addressed the media at a press conference that drew national attention, outlining his vision for the program. "I've built empires in music and business, and now I'm going to build a championship program here in Bowling Green," Combs declared. "I know people are skeptical, but I've surrounded myself with great football minds on my staff. We're going to bring an energy and swagger to this program that the MAC has never seen."

The hire has generated significant controversy given Combs' recent legal troubles and subsequent pardon. However, Bowling Green officials maintain that proper vetting was conducted and that Combs represents a unique opportunity for the university. With the season opener approaching, all eyes will be on the Falcons to see if Combs can translate his success in other industries to the gridiron.
