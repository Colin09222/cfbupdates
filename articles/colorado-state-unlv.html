Game Recap • January 29, 2026

UNLV Falls to Colorado State in 45-7 Beatdown

Mrs. Frank's Rams dominate in all phases, racking up 518 yards of total offense in season-opening rout

Colorado State's offense was unstoppable in their dominant victory over UNLV

Final Score

Colorado State 45
UNLV 7

FORT COLLINS, Colo. — It was a night to forget for UNLV and a night to remember for Colorado State. The Rams, under first-year head coach Mrs. Frank, delivered a comprehensive 45-7 demolition of the Rebels in their season opener, sending a clear message to the rest of the Mountain West that Colorado State means business in 2026.

The Rams' offense was virtually unstoppable from start to finish, amassing 518 yards of total offense while averaging an absurd 10.0 yards per play. Quarterback Jackson Brousseau was brilliant in his debut under the new coaching staff, completing 26 of 32 passes for 389 yards and four touchdowns. His accuracy and decision-making were impeccable, as he picked apart UNLV's secondary with surgical precision throughout the contest.

Colorado State's ground game was equally devastating. Running back Jalen Dupree bulldozed his way to 98 yards and two touchdowns on just 17 carries, consistently moving the chains and punishing UNLV's front seven. The Rams converted a remarkable 75% of their third-down attempts (3-4), keeping drives alive and the scoreboard ticking. The receiving corps was led by Kojo Antwi with 10 catches for 102 yards and a touchdown, while L. Avant added 3 receptions for 88 yards.

Defensively, Colorado State suffocated the Rebels, holding them to a meager 195 yards of total offense. UNLV managed just 73 yards on the ground and converted only 5 of 11 third-down opportunities. The Rebels' lone touchdown came in garbage time, a small consolation in what was otherwise a dismal performance for head coach Barry Odom's squad.

For Mrs. Frank, the victory couldn't have been more emphatic. The new head coach's aggressive offensive philosophy was on full display, and the players clearly bought into the system. If this performance is any indication, Colorado State could be a dark horse contender in the Mountain West this season.

"That's the standard we're setting here. We came out with intensity, we executed at a high level, and we played complete football. This is just the beginning. These players worked incredibly hard this offseason, and tonight was a reflection of that commitment."

— Mrs. Frank, Colorado State Head Coach
