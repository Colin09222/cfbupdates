Game Recap • January 29, 2026

Mississippi State's Jacob Clark Throws for Over 400 Yards Despite 27-21 Loss to FIU

Bulldogs quarterback puts up monster numbers in heartbreaking season-opening defeat

Mississippi State quarterback Jacob Clark fires a pass against FIU

Final Score

FIU 27
Mississippi State 21

STARKVILLE, Miss. — Jacob Clark did everything in his power to will Mississippi State to victory, throwing for a nation-leading 435 yards in the season opener. But it wasn't enough, as FIU stunned the Bulldogs 27-21 in a game that will leave Mississippi State fans wondering what could have been. Despite Clark's heroics through the air, costly turnovers and defensive breakdowns doomed the home team.

Clark completed 29 of 62 passes at a 46% clip, tossing two touchdowns against one interception. He was under siege for much of the afternoon, absorbing seven sacks as the offensive line struggled to contain FIU's pass rush. Still, he showed remarkable poise and arm talent, keeping the Bulldogs in the game despite the circumstances. His 435 passing yards currently lead all FBS quarterbacks through Week 0.
