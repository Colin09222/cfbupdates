Kirk Suspended for Bowl After Charging "DEI" Honorary Touchdown — NDSU Falls to New Mexico 43-41 in Five Overtimes

CU
By CFB Updates Staff July 9, 2026 · 8:00 PM ET
7 min read

It was, for fifty-nine minutes and forty-three seconds, the signature win of Charlie Kirk's coaching life. No. 16 North Dakota State, playing for a Mountain West Championship and a shot at revenge against the only team that had beaten them all year, led New Mexico 28-25 with 17 seconds on the clock. One stop. One knee-down of a prayer. That's all the Bison needed to hoist a trophy and bury the ghost of their season-opening loss.

They got neither. On the game's final snap of regulation, No. 16 NDSU — inexplicably, catastrophically — let a receiver run free behind the entire secondary and gave up a bomb, allowing New Mexico to complete a chunk play, spike, and burn a timeout at their own 42-yard line. What followed was one of the great heists in league history: a 62-yard field goal, straight down the pipe, to force overtime with no time left. Fargo went silent. Coach Kirk, per multiple eyewitnesses, made a sound "like a kettle."

Final/5OT — Mountain West Championship

New Mexico 43, NDSU 41 (5OT) — Lobos win the MWC

Regulation: UNM 25 · NDSU 28. NDSU led with :17 left, surrendered a bomb, allowed a 62-yard FG to force OT.

NDSU finishes 12-2 (both losses to New Mexico), ranked No. 18.

Five overtimes. It went five overtimes. And here is where the evening left the realm of football entirely and entered the realm of legend.

Between overtime possessions, with the stadium at a fever pitch, New Mexico's staff called a pre-arranged ceremonial play: an honorary touchdown for a special-needs child, a walk-in score that all parties understood was a moment of sportsmanship and would not count toward the game. Both benches had signed off. It was supposed to be the feel-good clip of the year.

Coach Kirk did not get the memo. Or he got the memo, read it upside down, and set it on fire.

"They put SEVEN on the board and NOBODY on my defense moved. That's a free touchdown. That's DEI football. NOT ON MY WATCH."

Witnesses say Kirk sprinted onto the field mid-ceremony, screaming about "an illegal uncontested score," and attempted to physically engage the honorary ball-carrier before being fended off by a wall of New Mexico players who formed a protective ring around the child. Officials, several New Mexico assistants, and eventually three members of NDSU's own strength staff converged. Kirk was ejected on the spot and, per the league office this morning, has been suspended for the bowl game.

62 YARDS The New Mexico field goal that forced OT — after NDSU gave up a bomb with :17 left leading 28-25

With Kirk banished to the locker room, play-calling for all five overtime possessions fell to NDSU assistant head coach Kim Jong Un, a man of many talents whose command of the English football lexicon remains, charitably, a work in progress. The Bison offense — leaderless, confused, and increasingly unable to decode their own coordinator — sputtered just enough. New Mexico punched in the winning two-point conversion in the fifth overtime. 43-41. Season over. Trophy gone.

Two-time Heisman winner DJ Scott, who ran and caught for a combined 200-plus yards in a losing effort, was asked at the podium what it was like taking signals from Coach Un in the biggest moment of the year. He stared into the middle distance for a long time before answering.

"I can't ever understand that nigga. Deadass. He said 'Rew Be Raw.' The fuck does that mean? He was tryna say QB DRAW the whole god damn time." — DJ Scott

"Five overtimes, bro. FIVE. And every single call he's yelling 'REW BE RAW, REW BE RAW' and pointing at me," Scott continued, palms up. "I thought it was a new package. I thought it was, like, a formation. I'm out here running routes to a play that don't exist. Turns out he wanted a QB draw. The whole time. Just say draw, man. Say. Draw."

Kirk, still ejected but very much available for comment, held an impromptu press conference in the tunnel that ran roughly eleven minutes and touched on no fewer than fourteen unrelated grievances.

"We didn't lose this game. We had it STOLEN by DEI, by the liberals, and by the fact that a man who calls a QB draw 'Rew Be Raw' was allowed to coach my football team in a CHAMPIONSHIP."

"Let's review the tape," Kirk said, gesturing at a tape that did not exist. "Number one: they scored a touchdown that my players were TOLD not to defend. That is affirmative action for the end zone. Number two: a sixty-two-yard field goal is not a real thing that happens in real football, that is a woke miracle, that was manufactured in a lab in San Francisco. And number three — and this is the big one — the moment I got kicked out, this team was handed over to a special-teams-and-morale coach who does not speak the language of freedom. We had the game won. We had it WON. And then democracy did what democracy does."

Asked if he understood that the honorary touchdown never counted, Kirk paused. "...It counted in my heart, and my heart is undefeated."

Postgame Q&A

Q: Coach, you led 28-25 with 17 seconds left. What happened on that last play?

A (Kirk): "Nothing happened. That's the point. NOTHING was supposed to happen and something happened anyway. My corner had one job: stand there. He did not stand there. He is now standing there permanently, on the bench, forever."

Q: A 62-yard field goal is a big-time kick. Credit to New Mexico?

A (Kirk): "I will credit New Mexico when the FBI credits New Mexico. That ball defied physics. I've spoken to two scientists and one guy at the gas station and all three agree: impossible."

Q: Coach Un, walk us through the overtime play-calling.

A (Kim Jong Un): "Is veruh simple. I say REW BE RAW. Everyone know REW BE RAW. Is the most famous play. DJ look at me like I am speaking Korean. I AM NOT SPEAKING KOREAN. I am speaking football. Rew. Be. Raw. Very clear. Not veruh guh he did not run it."

Q: DJ, did you ever figure out what he was calling?

A (DJ Scott): "In the FIFTH overtime, bro. Fifth. It clicked in the fifth. QB draw. I turned to Reynolds like 'he's been saying draw' and Reynolds goes 'I KNOW' — like HE knew and didn't tell ME? We lost that game in translation. Literally."

Q: Coach Kirk, you're suspended for the bowl. Your message to the team?

A (Kirk): "The bowl is a distraction from the real prize, which is the audit. I'll be watching from home, filing complaints with agencies, at least one of which is real. Beat whoever they put in front of you, and if the other team scores a touchdown you were told not to defend — you defend it anyway. Freedom isn't ceremonial."

Q: Any regrets about charging the honorary play?

A (Kirk): "My only regret is that I didn't get there faster."

And so North Dakota State's season ends the way it began — losing to New Mexico, twice bitten by the same fangs, a 12-2 record that in any other timeline is a triumph and in this one is a war crime tribunal. The Bison finish ranked No. 18, their coach suspended, their star running back emotionally exhausted, and their assistant head coach fully convinced that everyone else is the problem.

Rew, as they say, be raw. 🏈🦬
