Charlie Kirk Hired as Head Coach of University of Wyoming Football

Conservative commentator makes unexpected leap to college football, promises philosophical shift for Cowboys program

Charlie Kirk shakes hands with Wyoming Athletic Director during his introductory press conference

LARAMIE, Wyo. — In one of the most surprising coaching hires in recent college football history, the University of Wyoming has named Charlie Kirk as the next head coach of its football program. Kirk, best known as the founder of Turning Point USA and conservative political commentator, has no prior coaching experience but convinced the athletic department he was the right man to lead the Cowboys into a new era.

The hire comes after Wyoming parted ways with former head coach Craig Bohl following a disappointing 4-8 season in 2024-25. Bohl, who had been with the program since 2014 and led the Cowboys to multiple bowl appearances, saw the team struggle with roster turnover and inconsistent play on both sides of the ball. The program lost several key contributors to the transfer portal, leaving significant holes to fill heading into 2026.

Kirk addressed the media at his introductory press conference, outlining a vision that he described as a return to fundamental football values. "We're going to change the philosophy here," Kirk stated. "The previous staff ran a progressive offensive scheme that I believe strayed from what Wyoming football should be about. We're going to be more conservative in our approach—run the football, play great defense, win the turnover battle. That's how you win in the Mountain West."

The reaction across college football has been mixed, with some questioning Kirk's qualifications and others praising Wyoming for thinking outside the box. What's certain is that all eyes will be on Laramie when the Cowboys kick off their season, with Kirk's coaching debut one of the most anticipated storylines of the 2026 campaign.
