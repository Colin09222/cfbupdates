Coach Kirk Thanks "Fans, God and Israel" After Anonymous NIL Windfall Fuels NDSU's Historic Rise

CU
By CFB Updates Staff July 6, 2026 · 4:45 PM ET
5 min read

It has been quite the year in Fargo. North Dakota State, freshly promoted from the FCS ranks to the big lights of FBS, was widely expected to spend 2026 getting acquainted with life among the grown-ups — take the lumps, learn the level, live to fight another season. Instead, under first-year head coach Charlie Kirk, the Bison went 12-2, won their conference championship, and finished the year ranked #15 in the final AP poll. A debut for the ages.

And on Monday, Kirk finally addressed the not-at-all-mysterious force behind the meteoric rise: a massive, entirely anonymous NIL donation that transformed the program's recruiting overnight. Flanked by the NDSU logo, an Israeli flag, and last season's national runner-up hardware gleaming in the corner, Kirk shook hands with a silver-haired benefactor whose identity the program insists it simply cannot disclose.

10 → 35 Recruits signed before → after the anonymous donation (12 of them Hebrew transfers from Israel)

"I am very thankful for the anonymous donation and want to thank our fans, God and Israel," Kirk said, beaming beneath a MAGA "47" cap, still gripping the donor's hand warmly. "This is a blessing. A miracle, really. We were going to sign ten kids. Ten! And then — through the grace of forces I cannot name and would never speculate about — we signed thirty-five. You do the math. That's twenty-five extra blessings."

Asked whether he knew the identity of the donor, Kirk pressed a hand to his heart. "Absolutely not. Never met the man. Couldn't pick him out of a lineup. I don't even know what country he might hypothetically be the Prime Minister of. That would be inappropriate to know." At that exact moment the unnamed gentleman leaned toward the microphone and said, "Charlie, it is good to see you again, my friend," before an NDSU staffer physically wheeled the podium several feet to the left.

Recruiting class: 35 signees (up from a projected 10) · 12 international transfers from Israel

The 12 Hebrew transfers have already made waves in Fargo, where the average January temperature bears no resemblance whatsoever to the Mediterranean. "They're tough kids. Great fundamentals. Incredible special teams instincts," Kirk said. "And they brought a wonderful energy to the locker room. Also several of them keep asking who's paying for all this, and I keep telling them: God. And also possibly a friend of God. That's all I can say."

The transformation on the field was undeniable. The Bison, picked to finish near the bottom of their new conference, instead bulldozed their way to a league title and a top-15 ranking, announcing themselves as one of the great overnight FCS-to-FBS success stories in league history. Whether that success was built on coaching, culture, or an untraceable sack of cash handed over in front of a national flag, Kirk was adamant about where the credit belongs.

Press Conference Transcript

Q: Coach, can you tell us anything at all about the anonymous donor?

"I can tell you he is generous. I can tell you he is anonymous. I can tell you he is roughly this tall, has a distinguished silver head of hair, a firm handshake, and a strong opinion on regional security. Beyond that? Total stranger. Never seen him. Next question."

Q: You went from a projected 10 commits to 35. How does that even happen?

"Faith. Faith happens. You plant ten seeds, you pray, and the good Lord — working through channels I am legally and spiritually forbidden from identifying — returns thirty-five. That's a 250% blessing rate. You won't find those numbers at Alabama. Roll that."

Q: Twelve of the new recruits are Hebrew transfers from Israel. Walk us through that pipeline.

"Beautiful young men. Elite. We call it the Promised Land Pipeline. Fully organic. Completely coincidental. It just so happens that shortly after a very generous anonymous gift, twelve tremendous athletes flew in from a certain nation in the Middle East. Football is a mysterious game. God works in mysterious ways. So do certain heads of state I cannot name."

Q: The donor just called you "my friend" into the microphone. Care to respond?

"I heard nothing. That was the wind. Fargo gets a lot of wind. Sometimes the wind sounds like a warm, accented voice reminiscing about a shared history. That's meteorology. Go Bison."

Q: Any concerns about NIL compliance with a gift this size?

"Compliance loves us. We are the most compliant program in America. Everything is documented, everything is above board, everything came from an anonymous source who does not exist and whom I have never met and who is, if he existed, extremely law-abiding. I'd stake my MAGA hat on it. And I love this hat."

Q: So who do you want to thank for this incredible season?

"Our fans. God. And Israel. In that order — no, wait, God, then Israel, then fans. No. You know what, it's a three-way tie. They all know what they did. Especially the middle one. Go Bison."

As the press conference wound down, Kirk raised the runner-up trophy toward the cameras, then gestured warmly one final time at the "anonymous" man beside him. "None of this happens without belief," he said, voice thick with emotion. "Belief in your players. Belief in your program. And belief in a mystery donor whose name I will take to my grave and also do not know."

The unnamed benefactor smiled, gave a small wave to the assembled media, and mouthed the words "call me." Kirk, blinking back tears, mouthed back "who are you." It was, by all accounts, the most moving moment in NDSU football history. 🏈🇮🇱
