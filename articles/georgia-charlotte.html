Game Recap • January 29, 2026

#4 Georgia Skids By Charlotte 43-28

Bulldogs survive 49ers' upset bid as questions emerge about defensive performance

Georgia celebrates a touchdown against Charlotte at Sanford Stadium

Final Score

#4 Georgia 43
Charlotte 28

ATHENS, Ga. — Georgia escaped with a 43-28 victory over Charlotte in a game that was far closer than most expected. The fourth-ranked Bulldogs were pushed to the limit by the 49ers, who hung around despite being heavy underdogs. While a win is a win, head coach Kirby Smart will have plenty of concerns heading into next week after his defense surrendered 28 points to a Group of Five opponent.

The Georgia offense did its part, piling up impressive numbers behind quarterback Gunner Stockton, who threw for 376 yards and two touchdowns while completing 55% of his passes. Running back Cam Cook was dominant on the ground, rushing for 169 yards and two touchdowns on 27 carries, including a long run of 34 yards that sparked the Bulldogs' offense in the second half.

But it was the defensive performance—or lack thereof—that will concern Kirby Smart. Charlotte moved the ball effectively against a unit that returned several key starters from last season. The 49ers scored 28 points and kept the game competitive well into the fourth quarter, an unexpected development for a Georgia defense that prides itself on being one of the nation's best.

Defensively, Austin Romaine led the team with 6 tackles, while Vazell Payne added an interception that helped stem the tide in the third quarter. But the overall effort was inconsistent, with Charlotte finding soft spots in coverage and running lanes that shouldn't exist against a top-five team. It's early in the season, but these are issues that need to be corrected before conference play begins.

Georgia improves to 1-0 on the season, but the manner of victory leaves more questions than answers. Next week's opponent will certainly be watching this film and looking to exploit similar weaknesses. For Smart, the focus now shifts to correcting mistakes and getting his defense back to championship-caliber performance.

"A win is a win, and I'm proud of our guys for finding a way. But we've got a lot of work to do. We can't give up 28 points to anybody, much less a team we should be handling comfortably. We'll get back to work tomorrow and fix the issues. That's on me and the coaching staff."

— Kirby Smart, Georgia Head Coach
