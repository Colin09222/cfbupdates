Coach Thad Castle MURDERS FCS West 56-17 in Absolute Demolition

Absolute carnage in Fort Collins. Colorado State, under the iron fist of head coach Thad Castle, put together one of the most dominant performances of the young season, obliterating FCS West 56-17 in a game that was over before halftime. The Rams showed no mercy, and frankly, FCS West should probably just be grateful they made it out of Canvas Stadium alive.

Quarterback Jackson Brousseau was absolutely surgical, completing 27 of 33 passes for 316 yards and 5 touchdowns with just 1 interception. That's an 81% completion rate for those of you keeping score at home. Brousseau carved up the overmatched FCS West secondary like it personally offended him.

Final Score

The ground game was equally devastating. Jalen Dupree carried the rock 25 times for 164 yards and a score, while L. Avant added 7 carries for 98 yards and 2 touchdowns. When you can throw for 316 and run for 252 in the same game, there's not much the opposing defense can do except pray for halftime.

Coach Thad Castle has this team playing with an aggression that borders on illegal. The Rams are now 2-1 on the season and look like they could make serious noise in the Mountain West.
