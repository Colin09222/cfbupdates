Week 3 Recap · Bison Football

Coach Kirk Leads NDSU to 3-0 Start in First Season of Transition to FBS

CU
By CFB Updates Staff July 2, 2026 · 9:00 PM ET
5 min read

Nobody in Fargo is asking questions anymore. Not about the head coach's five offseason identities, not about the assistant who arrived with his own portrait for the weight room — and definitely not about the win column. North Dakota State is 3-0.

The Bison stayed perfect Thursday night with a commanding 28-7 takedown of Sacramento State, dropping the Hornets to 2-2 and announcing — loudly, in green and gold — that Coach Kirk's leap to the FBS is off to a flying start. NDSU outgained Sac State 336 to 216, held the Hornets to a jaw-dropping minus-4 rushing yards, and never trailed.

Final Score · Week 3

North Dakota State 28, Sacramento State 7

NDSU (3-0) · Sacramento State (2-2)

Senior quarterback Natean Hayes was efficient and explosive, completing 25 of 37 passes for 212 yards and three touchdowns (with two picks he'd probably like back). Hayes now sits at a 63% completion rate on the year with 715 yards and a 9-to-5 TD-to-INT line — good enough that his own assistant coach is already thinking about his next chapter.

"Get ready to rearn RinkedIn, buddeh."— Assistant Coach Kim Jong Un, on senior QB Natean Hayes

DJ Scott: The Freak in the Backfield

The headliner remains sophomore workhorse HB DJ Scott, who added 18 carries for 81 yards and — incredibly — 12 catches for 77 yards and a touchdown out of the backfield. On the season he's now leading the entire league in rushing with 607 yards on 56 carries, plus 6 rushing touchdowns, 3 receiving touchdowns, and 169 receiving yards. He currently sits 3rd in the Heisman polling.

607 Rushing yards — DJ Scott, league leader (3rd in Heisman race)

Coach Kirk, never one to undersell a talking point, went to bat for his star back.

Press Conference Transcript

Q: DJ Scott is putting up video-game numbers. What makes him so special?

"Kid's a freak. I mean look at him. He was meant to be a halfback. AND I MEAN BY MERIT. NOT BY THE COLOR OF HIS SKIN. Next question. Actually — did everybody write down the merit part? Write down the merit part."

Q: Coach Un, your defense held Sacramento State to negative-four rushing yards. How?

"Negative yard! Is beautifur. They run backward. We make them run backward. In my country, offense that go backward is arrested. Here, is just Tuesday. Redge McCaslin, he big rite man — no fat, ALL muscre. He work in rabor camp when he done. I mean… he work HARD. Very hard. Go Bison."

Q: Coach Kirk, three straight wins in your first FBS season. Surprised?

"Surprised? No. Vindicated? Absolutely. People said 'Charlie, you can't just walk in from an ayahuasca ceremony with Kim Jong Un as your DC and win football games.' And yet." *gestures at scoreboard* "And YET."

Q: Coach Un, any message for QB Natean Hayes as a senior?

"Natean, you senior now. Time is short. Get ready to rearn RinkedIn, buddeh. Update the profire. 'Open to work.' Add me as connection. I endorse you for skiirs — 'footbarr,' 'readership,' 'not throwing interception.' You throw two today. We work on that one. Go Bison."

Vanderpool, McCaslin Anchor a Suffocating Front

The Bison defense was the real story. Defensive tackle Zace Vanderpool was unblockable, piling up 3.0 sacks and four tackles for loss while living in the Sac State backfield. He wasn't alone — redshirt terror Kelton McCaslin, who leads the team on the season with 4 sacks, 9 TFL, and 12 solo tackles, added another sack, and D. Givance sealed the tone early with an interception and a 20-yard return.

"HE BIG RITE MAN. NO FAT, ALL MUSCLE. HE WORK IN LABOR CAMP WHEN HE DONE."— Assistant Coach Kim Jong Un, on Kelton McCaslin
