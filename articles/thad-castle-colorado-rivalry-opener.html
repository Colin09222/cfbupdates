Rocky Mountain Showdown · Season 2 Preview

THE BORDER WAR IS BACK: Castle's Champion Rams Open Year Two Against Hated Colorado

CU
By CFB Updates Staff July 6, 2026 · 6:15 PM ET
4 min read

The confetti from the PAC-12 Championship has barely been swept up in Fort Collins — and the schedule-makers already handed Coach Thad Castle a gift wrapped in pure hatred. The defending champion Colorado State Rams open Season 2 with the one game that means everything on this side of the Rockies: the Rocky Mountain Showdown against their biggest rival, the Colorado Buffaloes.

One state. Two schools. A hundred miles of pure, boiling contempt. And a first-year champion who has apparently been circling this date on his calendar with a Sharpie the size of a baseball bat since the second the confetti fell.

Season 2 · Week 1 — The Rocky Mountain Showdown

Colorado State (defending PAC-12 champions) vs Colorado

Rivalry Game · Border War

Head Coach: Thad Castle (Year 2)

365 Days Castle says he's been waiting to "hit somebody in black and gold"

A Champion With Something to Prove — Again

You'd think a 12-1 season and a conference crown would let a man relax. Not Castle. If anything, the ring made him hungrier. Sources inside the Rams facility say he's had the Colorado helmet taped to the blocking sled all offseason, and he refers to the Buffaloes exclusively as "the cattle up the road."

Opening the title defense against your archrival isn't a schedule quirk to Castle. It's a statement game. Win it, and the whole league knows the champs are back and meaner than ever. Lose it, and the entire state of Colorado never lets him hear the end of it.

"They call it the Border War. GOOD. I don't do exhibition games. I do WAR. And I've never lost one that started with me screamin'."— Thad Castle

Why This One Hits Different

Rivalry games throw the record book out the window. Rankings don't matter. Last year doesn't matter. All that matters is who wants it more for sixty minutes of organized violence between two schools that genuinely can't stand each other. For a personality like Castle — a man who wins the pregame, the sideline, AND the postgame — there is no bigger stage than the one where the whole state is watching and half of it wants him to lose.

The Rams bring the swagger of champions. Colorado brings a full season of watching CSU hoist a trophy and stew about it. That's a powder keg. Somebody's lighting it Week 1.

Press Conference Transcript

Q: Coach, opening the title defense against your biggest rival — how do you approach it?

"Approach it? I've been ASLEEP dreamin' about it. You give a defending champ his rival in Week 1, that's not a game, that's a GIFT. I'm gonna unwrap it slow, then I'm gonna run HALF BACK TOSS down their throats till the cows come home. And they're cattle, so — they'll come home."

Q: Colorado's fanbase says you're overrated after one good year.

"One good year? I went TWELVE AND ONE and won the whole conference. If that's overrated, sign me up for overrated forever, baby. I'll take my overrated trophy back to my overrated championship office and cry about it. Actually — did they really say that? Write down who said that."

Q: Any message for the Buffaloes before kickoff?

"HOLD YOUR COCKS AND PLAY FOOTBALL. That's it. That's the message. Oh — and elevation is still my sixth man. See you at altitude, cattle. Love ya. Mean it. Gonna hurt ya."

The Bottom Line

A defending champion. A blood rival. Week 1 of the title defense. You could not script a better opener if you tried. The Rocky Mountain Showdown is back, the champs are the hunted now, and Thad Castle is doing exactly what Thad Castle does best — turning the volume up to a level that shakes the whole mountain range.

Buckle up, buddehs. Season 2 doesn't ease you in. It kicks the door down. 🏈🐏
