Coach Kirk Selects NDSU as Coach Un Joins Him: "I Have Reary Guh Feerinf About This"

CU
By CFB Updates Staff July 2, 2026 · 2:30 PM ET
4 min read

The wait is over. After weeks of speculation, ayahuasca-fueled soul-searching, and at least one Commitment Day involving five different hats, Charlie Kirk has made it official: he is the new head coach of the North Dakota State Bison. And standing beside him at Thursday's introductory press conference, grinning ear to ear in matching NDSU green, was his hand-picked assistant coach — Kim Jong Un.

The room fell silent. Reporters exchanged glances. A camera flash popped. And through it all, Kirk looked genuinely baffled that anyone found the pairing remarkable. "I have reary guh feerinf about this," Kirk said, beaming, apparently having picked up his new assistant's cadence sometime over the offseason. "Best staff hire in the country. I don't know why everyone keeps asking me about it. He's a football guy."

When one reporter gently pointed out that Kim Jong Un is, in fact, the Supreme Leader of North Korea, Kirk waved a dismissive hand. "So? Bill Belichick had a résumé too. You people are obsessed with labels. I see a coach. I see a leader. I see a man who understands discipline, structure, and running the same play until the defense breaks. That's Bison football, baby."

Un, for his part, appeared delighted throughout, occasionally leaning into the microphone to add commentary that the assembled press struggled to parse. Athletic department staff described the new assistant as "shockingly hands-on," noting he had already installed a portrait of himself in the team weight room and renamed the practice bubble "The Glorious Dome of Unstoppable Victory."

Sources within the program say Un will oversee "special teams and morale," a role he reportedly negotiated up from "special teams" after a forty-minute monologue that ended in a standing ovation from a room he had personally instructed to give a standing ovation.
