Game Recap • January 29, 2026 • Dublin, Ireland

Kansas State Defeats Iowa State 47-28 in Dublin Opener

The Wildcats dominate on the ground with 222 rushing yards and five touchdowns in convincing international victory

Kansas State quarterback surveys the field against Iowa State defenders in Dublin

Final Score

#16 Kansas State 47
#17 Iowa State 28

DUBLIN, Ireland — Kansas State delivered a statement performance in the Dublin Opener, dismantling Iowa State 47-28 in a game that showcased the Wildcats' dominant rushing attack and opportunistic defense. The victory marks a strong start to the season for head coach Chris Klieman's squad, who entered the matchup as slight underdogs against the Cyclones.

The Wildcats wasted no time establishing their ground game, pounding Iowa State's defensive front for 222 yards on 45 carries with five rushing touchdowns. Kansas State's offensive line opened massive holes throughout the contest, averaging 4.9 yards per carry and controlling the clock for much of the afternoon. The balanced attack kept the Cyclones' defense on their heels, unable to key on any single aspect of the Kansas State offense.

Iowa State quarterback Rocco Becht put up impressive numbers in a losing effort, completing 25 of 41 passes for 373 yards and three touchdowns. Wide receiver Daniel Jackson was his favorite target, hauling in 10 receptions for 199 yards. However, two interceptions proved costly, as Kansas State's defense converted both turnovers into points, swinging momentum decisively in the Wildcats' favor.

The game was tight through the first half, with Kansas State holding a 30-10 advantage heading into the break. But the Wildcats put the game away in the fourth quarter, outscoring Iowa State 14-10 to seal the victory. The defensive performance was particularly impressive, as Kansas State limited the Cyclones to just 46 rushing yards on 20 attempts, forcing Iowa State into predictable passing situations.

With the win, Kansas State establishes itself as a legitimate Big 12 contender entering the 2026 season. The Wildcats will look to build on this momentum as they return stateside, while Iowa State must regroup quickly after a disappointing showing on the international stage. For Matt Campbell and the Cyclones, it's back to the drawing board as they search for answers to a defensive performance that allowed nearly 450 yards of total offense.
